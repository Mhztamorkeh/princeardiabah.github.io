The story behind the systems
Most automation builders come from tech. I came from people.

I spent years working across business strategy, marketing, UI/UX, and youth advocacy — in boardrooms, communities, and NGO war rooms across Ghana, the UK, Germany, the US, and Canada. I watched brilliant organisations fail not because of bad ideas, but because of broken systems: manual processes, data silos, and the quiet cost of doing things by hand.

That frustration — watching capable people waste hours on tasks that should run themselves — is what pulled me toward automation. Not as a hobby. As a calling.

My background in marketing and UI/UX means I don't just build workflows — I design experiences. My years in community management and youth advocacy, mentoring 500+ young professionals, taught me how to listen for the real problem underneath the stated one. And my consulting work across four continents gave me the systems-thinking muscle to see how pieces connect — and where they break.

Today, when I build an automation, I'm not just mapping nodes. I'm applying everything — the strategist, the community builder, the advocate — to create something that actually changes how people work.
